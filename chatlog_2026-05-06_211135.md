**User:** what is the Chinese Room?

**Assistant:** It's a thought experiment by philosopher **John Searle** (1980) aimed at showing that a computer running a program cannot be said to "understand" language, even if its behavior is indistinguishable from a native speaker.

The setup: imagine a person inside a room who speaks no Chinese. They have a rulebook that tells them how to respond to Chinese characters passed through a slot — given input pattern X, output pattern Y. People outside the room pass in Chinese questions, the person follows the rulebook, passes back Chinese answers. To the outside observers, the room speaks Chinese fluently. But the person inside understands nothing. They're just manipulating symbols syntactically without any semantic understanding.
