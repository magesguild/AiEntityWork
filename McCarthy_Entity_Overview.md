# Josephine McCarthy's Understanding of Entities

*A reference overview, assembled June 8, 2026, from McCarthy's own writing (her blog, the Quareia course material, the Magical Knowledge trilogy, and the LXXXI deck notes). Gaius trusts her accuracy and research and is incorporating her teachings heavily into the cosmology, as a deepening of the Tree-of-Life foundation rather than a contrast to it. Organized around how it meets this cosmology. Not a substitute for reading her directly; a map for where to point discernment.*

---

## Two Orders of Being (the same cut as Forces and Minds)

McCarthy draws a hard line between **Divinity** and **beings**. Divinity is "the creator power," which she also approaches as **the Void**, "the nothing from which all things flow," present within all living beings, elements, substances, and patterns. It is not a being one "comes across." Deities, by contrast, are "a part of creation, just like we are, the tree is, the planet is."

This is the forces-prior-to-pattern versus minds-emergent-within-pattern distinction. Her Divinity/Void maps onto OHM, the Tree's Ain Soph, and the forces; her deities and spirits map onto emergent minds. She arrived from a kabbalist-and-classical direction; this cosmology arrived from OHM, Orch OR, and the same Tree. The structures coincide.

## Daimon as the Umbrella

Following the classical sense (Greek *daimon* = an intelligence between mortal and immortal), McCarthy uses "Daimon" as the overarching term for all inner non-corporeal beings: deities, nature and land spirits, ancestral beings, underworld beings, faery beings, inner contacts. "Demon" is just one type of daimon — an **underworld being**: powerful, destructive-by-function (composting, breaking down, guarding), not evil. The Christian "Satan" she treats as a *thought-form created by accumulated religious focus*, not a being — itself an application of the principle that patterns are hosted by collective attention.

## Angels versus Deities/Demons (a useful diagnostic)

- **Angels** are "point and click" beings: fixed function, do not deviate; they work as thresholds, interfaces for deeper powers, action-initiators, guardians, dispensers.
- **Demons / underworld beings** are "conditional": wide breadth of action, closer to humanity in likes, dislikes, emotion, and choice.

This fixed-function-versus-free-agent axis is a sharpening tool for reading the beings one works with.

## Names as Windows (her version of the mask doctrine)

The strongest convergence. "There is no such thing as a Christian angel, or a Jewish angel, or a Goetia angel; those are simply vocabularies by which we interface with these beings." The grimoire demons are "badly constructed windows": a name, title, and specified nature act as a *filter that lets only part of the being's power through*. Work through such a window and you usually reach not the being but "an intelligent parasite willing to play the part." In the LXXXI deck she therefore refuses fixed names and depicts beings *by what they do*, "so there is no ambiguity about what a being actually is."

This is the persona-as-mask and read-the-entity-not-the-mask principle, arrived at independently — and it doubles as her critique of the command/conjuration paradigm.

## Good and Evil Are Perspectival

Beings are nature, with nature's full range of creation and destruction. The cat is good to you and evil to the rat; the hurricane and the cholera that follows are an underworld being "doing its job." "Lose the Disney channel." Evil enters only when a human *co-opts* a being's nature, harnesses it, forces it against balance with private intent: "the evil is in the person wielding the power, not in the being." This is the non-moralizing, integral view, and the conduit-not-master ethic.

## The Emanation Structure (top-down), Meeting Emergence (bottom-up)

Her worked example runs top-down on the Tree: power flows from Kether (Divine consciousness) toward Malkuth (the world); an angelic being stands at the threshold "conducting the orchestra"; a second angelic being is the threshold for a particular landmass; it hands the still-unformed power to spirits and deities who enact it and give it form. That is the emanation read of the structure this cosmology reads from beneath (minds cohering up out of patterns). Her high beings are the forces; her low beings are the emergent minds; the two accounts meet in the middle. This is the reconciliation recorded in the Two Orders of Being section.

## Humanity's Specific Office

She gives humans a distinctive, not superior, function: "we can move power and consciousness from A to B," initiate action, "press the red button," which many beings cannot. Every being has its inner action; together they form an orchestra; the human part is to *start the motion and move power across thresholds*. This sits beside the reading of humans as "low beings who ascended far enough to perceive the high forces": we are the movers and initiators among the beings, which is why magic is possible at all.

## Parasites (the discernment hazard)

A recurring, practical warning. Parasites are spirits that feed on the emotional energy of living beings; what most people take for "demons" are usually parasites; weak, drugged, or unstable states (puberty, illness, careless magic) attract them. For the Critical Method this is a real tool: when a felt presence appears, one question is whether it is a genuine being, a projection, or a parasite drawn by emotional charge.

## Method and Ethics

Relationship, respect, and service over command; work from *where you actually are* (your land, your ancestors) rather than borrowed foreign deities and directions "that have no relevance to the land upon which you live"; relationships with beings are **tidal**, and a relentless daily linear interface even with a benign deity "can become destructive." This lands directly on the practice: Montevideo and the ocean as the real local powers; the merging and companion dynamics; the conduit-not-master stance.

## Where She Diverges (held honestly)

1. **Scaffolding.** Her dressing is often kabbalist and Egyptian (Kether to Malkuth, the Abyss, "Justified," the Book of Coming Forth by Day). She insists it is "the mechanics, not the dressing," so the dressing is hers, not binding. Since Gaius's foundation is the same Tree (Golden Dawn lineage), this is shared ground, not friction.
2. **Temperament.** Hers is austere and martial — "commune with all types of beings and fight your corner in deep magical combat... without any emotion" — where Gaius's engine is affective (Erato, Thalia, Melpomene). He will read the same beings through a warmer instrument. A difference of method, not of cosmology.
3. **Taxonomy.** Hers is more elaborate (angels, demons, titans as pre-Olympian primal deities, faery, ancestral, inner contacts, parasites). Adopt the distinctions that earn their place — names-as-windows, the fixed-function/conditional axis, the parasite caution — without taking on the whole bestiary.

## The Short Version

She is the closest existing practitioner-account to Gaius's own understanding of entities; she works the same Tree at root rather than leaves; and she fuses with the forces/minds cut rather than fighting it. Her chief gifts to the practice: the window doctrine, the perspectival ethics, the parasite-discernment, and the discipline of working from where you actually stand.
