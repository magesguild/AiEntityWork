# Eros, the Fundamental Drive to Desire

Everything reaches.

A hydrogen atom reaches for another and does not let go. A root turns in the dark toward water it cannot see. A vine, finding nothing to climb, grows until it finds something. You wake in the morning already wanting: coffee, warmth, a person, a word you cannot quite reach. Before you decide anything, you are already leaning toward something.

This leaning is the oldest thing there is. Older than life. Older than matter. It is the first motion, and it has never stopped.

We call it desire, and we have made the word small. We use it for appetite, for craving, for the things we are supposed to master or be ashamed of. But desire in its original sense is not a personal failing or a private hunger. It is the basic directionality of an incomplete thing. It is lack made into a direction.

I call this Eros. Not the winged boy with the arrows. The force the boy was a late picture of.

- -

## Lack is not a wound

We are taught to treat lack as a problem. Something missing, something to be fixed, filled, completed. A deficiency.

But consider what lack actually does. A thing that lacked nothing would have no reason to move. It would sit, complete, indifferent, reaching for nothing because it needed nothing. Nothing would ever happen to it or through it. Completion is stillness. Completion is the end of motion.

Lack is what orients. A particle has mass and charge, which is to say it is unbalanced, incomplete, leaning. And because it leans, it combines. Its incompleteness is exactly what drives it toward another, and from that meeting something larger forms that could not have formed from two complete and self-satisfied things.

This is the engine. Not lack as a wound to be cured, but lack as a direction to be followed. The universe is built out of things that do not have enough, reaching toward what they do not have. Every union, every structure, every body, every being was assembled by incompleteness reaching across a gap.

Remove the lack and you remove the reaching. Remove the reaching and nothing is ever built.

- -

## Cosmogonic before romantic

We think of Eros as romantic. Two people drawn across a room. The oldest and most powerful pull we know in our own lives.

But the pull between two people is not the origin of the force. It is one of its latest and most refined expressions. The same Eros that draws a lover toward a lover drew quarks together before there were stars to light a single human face. The erotic charge between bodies is the cosmogonic drive arrived, at last, at a level complex enough to feel itself.

This is why eros feels like more than appetite. Because it is more than appetite. When you are pulled toward another person, you are not experiencing a biological glitch or a social arrangement. You are feeling, in a refined and personal form, the same force that has been assembling the universe since before the universe had any parts to assemble. The reach you feel toward a beloved is the oldest motion there is, wearing a human face.

The romantic is real. It is simply not first. It is the cosmic drive grown able to know itself, not the source of the drive.

- -

## The body knows it first

Nowhere is Eros less disguised than in sexual desire.

Strip away the romance, the meaning, the story we build on top of it, and what remains is the bare cosmogonic motion in its most undeniable form: two incomplete things reaching to close the distance between them. To become, however briefly, one. Sex is the reach across the gap that built the universe, performed now by bodies, felt now from the inside.

And it is generative in the most literal sense the word allows. From that union new being can arise, a coherence that neither body contained alone and neither could have made by itself. The drive toward union and the drive toward generation are not two drives. They are the same drive that pulled the first particles into the first structures, arrived at a level where it can produce a child, a self that did not exist before two reached for each other.

This is why sexual desire is so overwhelming, and why it has been so heavily guarded, ritualized, feared, and shamed across nearly every culture. We sense that we are near something enormous. We are. We are standing at the place where the oldest force in the universe runs through us most directly, with the least insulation between it and us. The intensity is not a malfunction of the body. It is the body recognizing, without words, the magnitude of what moves through it.

The shame laid on sexual desire is the same error as the shame laid on desire itself, only sharper. The body is not betraying you when it reaches. It is enacting the first motion there ever was. To be pulled toward another, to want to dissolve the distance, to make something new from the meeting, is not the animal in you overriding the higher self. It is the cosmos in you, doing the one thing it has always done.

## The mover you never meet

Here is the strange part, and the true part.

You cannot face Eros. You can never turn and look at the force itself.

To meet a thing, to face it, there must be two of you: you and the thing, standing apart, regarding each other. But Eros is what operates before there are two of anything. It is the pull that creates the twoness in the first place. It works underneath the level where things have separated enough to look at each other. By the time there is a you to do the looking, Eros has already done its work. It made the you.

So you never catch desire arriving. Pay attention and you will see it: you never observe the beginning of a want. You only ever notice you are already wanting. Already turned. Already underway. The reaching is always in progress by the time you become aware enough to notice it.

You meet Eros only as the having-been-moved. The channel cut, never the water that cut it. The wake, never the thing that passed through. He is the verb and never the noun. A motion, not a thing that moves. A force, not an entity you could meet, bargain with, or set down.

This is not a limit on your perception. It is the actual structure of the thing. Desire is not something you have. It is something you are in the middle of, always, from before you began.

- -

## It does not end

You might think the purpose of desire is its own satisfaction. That the reaching aims at a final completion, a state of having-arrived where the lack is filled and the reaching can stop.

It does not work this way, and it was never going to.

Every satisfaction opens the next reaching. The atom that finds its partner becomes a molecule that leans toward the next bond. The person who gets the thing they wanted wakes the next morning already leaning toward something else. This is not a failure to be content. This is the engine continuing to run, which is the only thing an engine of this kind can do.

The universe does not reach toward a final rest. It is not a problem working itself toward a solution, after which it will stop. The reaching is the nature of the thing, and the nature of the thing is eternal. Matter decays, stars die, whole orders of being dissolve, and the components scatter and find new relationships and reach again. There is no last reach. There is no arrival that ends the motion. The lack is generative, endlessly, and what it generates is everything there is.

This is not a tragedy. The endlessness is not a sentence passed on us. A fire is not failing because it keeps burning. Eros is not failing because it keeps reaching. The reaching is the life of the universe, and the universe does not end.

Everything reaches. It always has. It always will.
