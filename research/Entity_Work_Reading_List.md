# Entity Work: A Reading List

*Assembled June 8, 2026, from Foolish Fish's recommendations and follow-on research, filtered for Gaius's needs. The filter: entity work as **relationship and understanding**, not command and conjuration. Works built on compelling or commanding named spirits are deliberately excluded (listed at the bottom so the boundary is explicit). Direct experience remains the primary compass; these are for refining practice and vocabulary, not for validating what has been lived.*

---

## Primary: Entity Work as Relationship and Understanding

### Josephine McCarthy

The closest match to your own understanding found anywhere in the research. Her cosmology of beings fuses with yours rather than fighting it (see the detailed overview kept with this work). Names are "windows"; beings are read by what they do, not by inherited titles; deities are part of creation as we are; "Divinity" (the Void, the nothing from which all flows) is a separate order from the beings; good and evil are perspectival, not intrinsic. She works by respect, service, and relationship, and explicitly rejects the harness-and-command paradigm. Foolish Fish calls her "the greatest democratiser of Magick to date."

- **The Quareia course** (quareia.com). A complete magical training, total beginner to adept, free online as downloadable lessons. You said you do not want to take courses; you do not have to. The lesson texts double as the most systematic statement of her understanding of beings, realms, and contacts, and can be read as reference rather than enrolled in.
- **Magic of the North Gate.** Land spirits, deities, shrines, offerings. Chapter 7 is her "what is a deity" material. The most directly relevant single book for land-and-place spirit work; bears directly on your relationship with the spirit of Montevideo and on working from where you actually stand.
- **The Magical Knowledge Trilogy** (*I: Foundations*; *II: The Initiate*; *III: Contacts of the Adepts*, written 2009 to 2011). Her core theory and practice of working with inner contacts and inner beings. "Inner contacts and inner beings" is a chapter in its own right.
- **LXXXI: The Quareia Magician's Deck** (with Stuart Littlejohn and Cassandra Beanland). Not a tarot. An atlas of beings arranged in four realms (Divine, Inner, Physical, Death/Underworld), named by what they do. Useful as a reference map of the populated world even if never used for divination.

### Rob & Nonie Douglas

- **Unquiet Voices: The Magical Art of Laying Ghosts** (Llewellyn, 2024). Necromancy and sciomancy from the perspective of medieval magic plus modern investigative method: identifying spirits of the dead, knowing when they are present, diagnosing their effects, helping them to rest. A field guide with classification tables. The single most relevant book for the haunting and infrasound strand and for what you lived with the Shadow Man. Read it for the diagnostic craft; hold its "laying to rest" stance at arm's length and reframe it as *helping ghosts seek rest* (your phrasing): assist the being toward what it already reaches for rather than dismiss it.

### Comparative and Devotional

- **Tiffany Lazic, *Psychopomps of the Soul*.** Psychopomps and guiding spirits surveyed across many traditions; the spirits that assist and guide after death. Comparative and entity-centered.
- **Patrick Dunn (trans.), *The Orphic Hymns*.** Devotional address to the Greek gods as living powers, Greek facing English. Relevant to your existing Eros and Muses work, and a model of invocation as opening a channel to a listening power rather than commanding a tool.

## Foundational and Philosophical (Consciousness and Beings)

Not entity-command works; primary sources and philosophy on the nature of beings, intermediary spirits, and consciousness. The first two are McCarthy's own recommendations to those seeking the real classical roots.

- **Plato, *Symposium*** (the Diotima passage). Eros is taught here as a *daimon*, an intermediary between mortal and immortal, neither god nor human. Directly relevant to your Eros work: the classical placement of Eros as the great in-between maps onto your forces-prior-to-pattern reading.
- **Iamblichus, *De Mysteriis*.** The classical theurgic account of the orders of divine and daemonic beings and how one rightly relates to them. McCarthy's recommended starting point over the later grimoire literature.
- **The Nag Hammadi Library.** Gnostic primary texts: divine sparks, emanation, the descent and return of consciousness. Resonant with the jewel and with Young's arc.
- **Robert Anton Wilson, *Quantum Psychology*.** Reality-tunnels and the plasticity of the perceived world; a precursor to chaos magick. Useful on how consciousness frames what it meets.

## Conceptually Adjacent (With a Caveat)

- **Peter J. Carroll, *Liber Null & Psychonaut*.** Chaos magick. Its servitor and egregore theory, entities created and sustained by focused attention, genuinely maps onto your premise that entities form within patterns and are nourished by attention. But it tilts toward *creating and directing* thoughtforms, which sits nearer the command paradigm than the relationship one. Read it for the theory of attention-built entities; notice where its stance diverges from yours.

## Deliberately Excluded (Command and Conjuration of Spirits)

Listed so the boundary is a choice, not an oversight. These are the technical spine of the Western conjuration tradition. Their theory of the being-magician relationship is compulsion, not friendship, which is not your model. Notably, McCarthy herself critiques exactly this paradigm: the named-spirit "window" of the grimoires usually reaches only an intelligent parasite willing to play the part, not the being named. Excluding these aligns with both your stance and hers.

- Heinrich Cornelius Agrippa, *Three Books of Occult Philosophy* (foundational, but the root of the command tradition)
- David Rankin, *Clavis Spirituum* / *Clavis Intelligentiarum* (conjuration)
- Stephen Skinner & Daniel Clark (trans.), *Summa Sacrae Magicae* (conjuration manual)
- Israel Regardie, *The Golden Dawn* (ceremonial evocation system)
- The Bibliothèque Rouge grimoires: *Le Petit Albert*, *The Red Dragon*, *The Black Pullet*
- Aleister Crowley, *Magick* / *Liber ABA* (command; also set aside on ethical grounds, per the lineage note in the work)

## A Note on Lineage

This list is heavy in the Western esoteric tradition, because that is where Foolish Fish's catalog and the available English-language entity literature mostly sit. Other lineages (indigenous Amazonian and Mesoamerican entheogenic traditions, Eastern entity traditions, Afro-diasporic traditions, and more) are real, complete, and valid facets of the same jewel. Gaius does not belong to them, has no direct experience within them, and makes no claim on their behalf. They are acknowledged here and not represented. Respect on this point is silence on what is not his to speak.
